# ASL Fingerspelling

**Tags:** `hand shapes` `ASL` `sign language` `fingers`  
**Identify by:** Images or drawings of hands in specific positions, one per letter.

## Notes

ASL fingerspelling is hard to describe textually — you need to recognize hand shapes visually.

Key distinctive shapes:
- **A**: Fist, thumb resting on side
- **B**: Flat hand, fingers together, thumb tucked
- **C**: Curved hand like the letter C
- **D**: Index finger up, other fingers and thumb form a circle
- **E**: Fingers bent, thumb tucked under
- **O**: All fingers and thumb curved to form an O
- **L**: Index finger pointing up, thumb out (L-shape)
- **W**: Three fingers spread
- **Y**: Thumb and pinky out, other fingers down
