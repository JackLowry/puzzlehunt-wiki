# Common AHA Moments

Patterns that appear repeatedly across puzzlehunts. If you're stuck, run through this list.

---

## "The answer is staring at you"

- **Read the first letters** of every clue, answer, or item
- **Read the last letters**
- **Read every other letter / every nth letter**
- **Read the lengths** of the answers as A1Z26

---

## Ordering Problems

"I have the right letters but they spell gibberish."

- Try alphabetizing the source items before extracting
- Try ordering by answer length
- Try ordering by the number in the clue
- Try ordering by the grid/spatial position (top-left → bottom-right)

---

## "The numbers don't match A1Z26"

- Numbers > 26: could be ASCII decimal — try `=CHAR(n)`
- Numbers in range 1–118: could be atomic numbers (periodic table)
- Numbers in range 1–7: could be days of week, musical notes, or rainbow colors
- Numbers that don't fit any range: might be coordinates, dates (MMDD), or phone numbers

---

## "It's an image / grid but I don't know what encoding"

- Count the cells: 26 = probably one letter per cell (alphabet grid)
- 2×3 or 3×2 = probably Braille cells
- Dots and dashes = Morse
- Filled/empty squares = could be binary or Braille
- Symbols in a tic-tac-toe grid = Pigpen

---

## "The flavor text is weird"

Flavor text in hunt puzzles almost always contains a hint:
- **Bolded / italicized words** → look at their first letters
- **Unusual capitalization** → might be an acrostic
- **Repeated phrases** → count or index them
- **Strange word choice** → might be a indicator for a wordplay type

---

## "I have the right answer but it's not accepted"

- Try dropping articles: "THE", "A", "AN"
- Try the plural / singular
- Try the past tense / base form
- Check if the hunt expects answers in a specific format

---

## Meta-puzzle Patterns

- Answers that are all in the same category → category name or member might be the meta answer
- Answers that each share a word with something → the shared words spell the answer
- Answers that can all have a word added before/after → the added word is the answer
- A grid of answers where something applies to rows vs. columns → combine the row-thing and column-thing

---

## The "Kansas City Shuffle"

When a puzzle is "too easy" and you got an answer quickly: double-check. Hunt puzzles sometimes have an obvious-but-wrong answer and a harder-to-see correct one.
